About Me

Advancing equitable transportation systems through rigorous methodology and engaged scholarship.

Dr. Mongkut Piantanakulchai is a transportation researcher and professor based in Thailand whose work integrates systems thinking, operations research, and field-based evidence. His scholarship centers on designing reliable bus services for rapidly growing cities and guiding public agencies toward measurable performance outcomes.

  • Expertise in transportation systems analysis and network design.
  • Specialist in bus network optimization and rerouting strategies.
  • Methodological focus on multi-criteria decision making, discrete choice modeling, and participatory research design.

Research Interests

Connecting geospatial analytics with technology acceptance to inform people-centered mobility policy.

Transportation Planning

Scenario planning for metropolitan bus and rail integration, emphasizing performance monitoring frameworks.

Bus Rerouting

Dynamic rerouting algorithms that balance coverage, equity, and carbon reduction goals.

Geospatial Analysis

High-resolution spatial analytics combining GTFS feeds, smart-card data, and remote sensing layers.

Technology Acceptance

Behavioral modeling of traveler adoption of digital ticketing, real-time information, and MaaS platforms.

Current Projects

Collaborative research translating theory into actionable policy guidance for Thai transit agencies.

Flagship Study

PTSE-Bus Rerouting Project

The PTSE initiative develops a decision-support environment for Bangkok's city bus services, optimizing route restructuring while maintaining neighborhood accessibility. The project blends field surveys, AVL feeds, and stakeholder co-design workshops to produce adaptable rerouting playbooks for municipal operators.

  • Integrates demand forecasting with operational constraints across 40+ corridors.
  • Deploys geospatial dashboards for real-time evaluation of service adjustments.
  • Provides policy briefs for the Ministry of Transport and metropolitan transit authorities.
